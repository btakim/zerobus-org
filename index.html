NON-COMMERCIAL RESEARCH INITIATIVE

Zero-emission intercity coach research for Europe

ZeroBus exists to accelerate the shift from diesel to truly zero-emission intercity travel. We compile operator insights, model routes and charging, and publish practical findings that help the sector run reliable, affordable electric coach services.

What ZeroBus is

ZeroBus is an independent, noncommercial research organization. We engage operators, OEMs, charging providers, and public authorities to test where electric coaches can meet headways, comfort, and cost targets — today and over the next few years.

Why ZeroBus exists

  • Shifting demand: A climate-aware generation already prefers sustainable options when the experience and price match. Intercity coaches can capture rail/air spillover if they go zero-emission without compromising convenience.
  • Policy momentum: The EU’s climate framework (e.g., Fit-for-55 / 2030 targets) pushes deep emissions cuts across transport. Data and demos help convert targets into workable operations on real corridors.
  • Product wave: Major OEMs are rolling out high-energy, long-range electric coaches in Europe over the next cycles (e.g., Daimler and MAN roadmaps). Planning now positions operators to pilot early and scale with the hardware.

Industry context

  • Operations: Energy planning must account for gradients, HVAC, dwell times, driver changes, and seasonal loads.
  • Charging: Depot CCS vs. en-route MCS; grid capacity, peak management, and tariff structures are decisive.
  • Economics: TCO hinges on utilization, leasing/financing, energy prices, maintenance, and incentives.
  • Customer experience: Quiet rides, Wi-Fi, and predictable schedules can win modal share if ticket prices remain competitive.

What we publish

  • Route and charging scenarios (200–500 km corridors).
  • Open summaries of interviews with operators and suppliers.
  • Transparent assumptions and sensitivity analyses.

Operator & supplier questions

These are the core questions we’re asking in our outreach. If you can share experience or data, we’d love to talk.

1) Current operations

  • What are your busiest intercity corridors (distance, headways, load factors)?
  • Typical dwell times, layovers, and driver changes on these routes?
  • Seasonal peaks and reliability constraints we should account for?

2) Fleet & procurement

  • Have you trialed or planned electric coaches yet? Which models or specs are you considering?
  • Leasing vs. buying preferences; contract lengths; residual value concerns?
  • What range/energy targets feel viable for your duty cycles?

3) Charging & infrastructure

  • Depot capacity today and grid upgrade timelines?
  • Appetite for en-route charging (MCS) vs. depot-only strategies?
  • Site constraints (space, permitting) and tariff structures you face?

4) Economics & incentives

  • How do you evaluate TCO vs. diesel on target corridors?
  • Which national/EU incentives materially change the business case?
  • What utilization/availability thresholds must an e-coach meet to be adopted?

5) Partnerships & data

  • Can we collaborate on anonymized data for modeling (ridership, timings, energy)?
  • What would a low-risk pilot look like (fleet size, duration, KPIs)?
  • Which stakeholders (DSOs, municipalities, hubs) should be at the table?

6) Feedback for ZeroBus

  • What research would be most useful for your decision-making?
  • Which assumptions should we stress-test first?
  • How would you like results shared (briefs, workshops, dashboards)?
