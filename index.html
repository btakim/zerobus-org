What is ZeroBus?

An independent research initiative that maps viable electric‑coach corridors in Europe and publishes practical guidance for operators, OEMs, charging providers, and public bodies.

Research only — we don’t sell tickets

Why ZeroBus?

  • Climate‑aware demand: Travelers will choose low‑carbon modes when reliability, comfort, and price align.
  • EU goals: Green Deal / Fit‑for‑55 push deep transport decarbonization; operators need data‑backed playbooks.
  • Product wave: New e‑coaches from European OEMs arrive through 2026; planning now enables early pilots.

Problem

Limited sustainable choices

Rail depends on long‑cycle infrastructure upgrades with uneven national progress; flights are fast but carbon‑intensive and costly on <500 km city pairs; diesel coaches remain cheap but polluting and slow.

Outdated customer experience

Across modes, travelers face spotty Wi‑Fi, limited power, and dated interiors — misaligned with modern expectations for connected work and leisure.

Why now

Technology

Next‑gen electric coaches with 500 + km range are rolling out from European OEMs (e.g., Daimler, MAN) through 2026.

Policy & incentives

EU and national frameworks support zero‑emission fleets and pilots, aligning public goals with operator economics.

Amenity leap

Reliable connectivity (e.g., satellite backhaul), USB‑C and Qi2 power at seat enable a meaningfully better onboard experience.

Research questions

Operations

Which 200–500 km corridors can meet headways and reliability with current/near‑term specs?

Charging & grid

What mix of depot CCS and en‑route MCS works by corridor given grid capacity and tariffs?

Economics

How does TCO compare vs. diesel under realistic utilization, financing, incentives, and maintenance?

Passenger experience

Which amenities (Wi‑Fi, power, seating, quietness) drive mode shift and willingness to pay?

Scaling path

What pilot design (fleet size, duration, KPIs) de‑risks expansion across multiple city pairs?

Method

Data modeling

Duty‑cycle energy models account for gradients, HVAC loads, dwell times, and seasonal effects.

Interviews

Structured conversations with operators, OEMs, charging providers, DSOs, and authorities.

Comparative analysis

Rail/air/coach time‑price‑CO₂ trade‑offs on target corridors.

Open summaries

We publish assumptions, sensitivity analyses, and non‑confidential results.
